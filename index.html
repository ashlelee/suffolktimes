Man Arrested After Major Car Accident on the Long Island Expressway

By Lisa Byline, Staff Reporter | Published: April 23, 2025

"Local Man's High-Speed Tesla Crash on LIE Leaves Three Hospitalized, Traffic in Chaos; Arrested on DUI Charges—Claims Autopilot Was Driving"

Photo: Suffolk County Tribune

An Asian man was taken into custody Tuesday afternoon following a multi-vehicle accident that caused major traffic delays on the Long Island Expressway, authorities confirmed.

According to the Suffolk County Police Department, the suspect, identified as 53-year-old Michael Chen, was driving eastbound at high speed when he lost control of his Tesla Model X near Exit 49, resulting in a four-car pileup. Witnesses at the scene reported seeing the vehicle swerving before it collided with another car, triggering a chain reaction that left several drivers injured.

Emergency services arrived within minutes, and three people were transported to nearby hospitals with life-threatening injuries. The driver, who appeared uninjured, was questioned on the scene and subsequently arrested.

Police stated that the arrest was made on suspicion of reckless driving and driving under the influence. Chen was taken into custody and booked at the 5th Precinct, where he is awaiting arraignment.

Investigators are reviewing dashcam footage and conducting interviews with witnesses. Authorities said that more charges could be filed pending toxicology results and further investigation.

Traffic on the LIE was backed up for several miles during the evening rush hour as crews worked to clear the scene. All lanes have since reopened.

Photo: Suffolk County Tribune

The incident has sparked outrage among local residents. "This reckless driving is getting out of hand," said local resident Gertrude O'Toole. "Every day I'm stuck in traffic because of these accidents."

Another resident, Mortimer Reilly, expressed concern over the impact on the community's safety. "This accident is devastating," Reilly lamented. "How are we supposed to feel safe when there are ambulances everywhere?"

Anyone with information about the incident is encouraged to contact Suffolk County Crime Stoppers.

"This is a tragedy for our community," said Suffolk County Police Chief Clarence Heitner."

Chen, who claims he was not at fault, insists that his Tesla's autopilot feature was driving at the time of the crash. "I was just sitting there, when suddenly the car decided to go rogue," he told reporters from his holding cell. "I think the autopilot malfunctioned."

Local authorities are now considering implementing a new law requiring all Tesla drivers to focus their eyes on the road to ensure they are not distracted during autopilot mode.
